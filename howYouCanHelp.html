How You Can Help Save Water

Yard and Garden

  • Adjust sprinklers so only your lawn is watered and not the house, sidewalk, or street.
  • Choose shrubs and groundcovers, instead of turf for hard to water areas such as steep slopes and isolated strips.
  • Plant during the spring or fall when the watering requirements are lower.
  • Minimize evaporation by watering during the early morning hours, when temperatures are cooler and winds are lighter.
  • Conserve water by adding a couple layers of mulch to your garden and landscape after deep watering the areas in the spring.
  • Classify your plants by knowing which ones use the same amount of water together.
  • Choose native plants for your yard and garden that would grow naturally in your climate.

Bathroom

Shower

  • Put a bucket in the shower while you're waiting for the water to warm up, and use the water you catch for watering plants, flushing the toilet or cleaning.
  • Turn off the water if you shave or brush your teeth in the shower to save time.
  • Spend less time in the shower, around one song's worth.
  • Install a low-flow shower head
  • Stick to 10 minute showers instead of baths

Sink

  • Turn off the water while you brush your teeth and shave.
  • Install low-flow faucet aerators.
  • Check for and fix any leaky faucets.

Toilet

  • Install a low-flow toilet.
  • Put a plastic bottle filled with water in your toilet tank to reduce the amount of water used per flush.
  • Put dye or food coloring into the tank to check for a leak. If it appears in the bowl without flushing, there's a leak.
  • "If it's yellow, let it mellow."
  • Don't flush things down the toilet to dispose of them, throw things away in a garbage bin.

Kitchen

  • Balance the amount of time you spend rinsing fruits and veggies. An apple under the tap is ok but a bunch of potatoes should have the sink filled half way up.
  • Don't use the dishwasher until it's full.
  • Rinse dishes in a half-filled sink rather than under a running tap.
  • Use a sink strainer and throw out what's caught instead of using the garbage disposal.
  • Don't defrost or melt ice under running water. Half fill a sink if you really need to.
  • Try steaming instead of boiling, it uses less water and the food retains for nutrients.
  • Turn off taps tightly and fix any dripping sinks.
  • Dry brush and spot clean the floor as opposed to mopping the whole floor.
